Langos (Lángos)

Description

Learn how to make langos, a tasty fried Hungarian bread that is best enjoyed warm. To simplify the recipe, instant yeast and a stand mixer is used to make the dough.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (280 grams)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • 1¼ teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup lukewarm milk (250 ml)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • sour cream or yoghurt
  • shredded cheese

Steps

  1. In a bowl of a stand mixer, combine flour, salt and sugar. Mix with a spoon. Add yeast. Mix again. Add milk and give it a quick mix with a spoon again.
  2. With bread attachment, mix on low speed for a few minutes (about 3-4) or until well combined (see note 4).
  3. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and clean kitchen towel. Let it proof in a warm place until doubled in size (45-60 minutes, see note 5).
  4. When ready, transfer the dough onto a well-floured work top. Divide into 4 equally-looking pieces.
  5. Form a ball out of each piece.
  6. Roll out with a rolling pin (about ¼-inch/0.5-centimeters thick and about 7-inch/17-centimeter in diameter).
  7. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil.
  8. When hot, add one rolled flatbread in. Fry for about a minute before turning it over (use kitchen tongs) and fry for a further minute.
  9. Langos should be nice golden brown, but some pale spots are fine.
  10. Place each fried bread onto a few sheets of paper kitchen towel to get rid of any excess oil.
  11. Spread each with a mixture of crushed garlic & olive oil.
  12. Top with sour cream and shredded cheese.
